Healthy Lifestyle

Adopting healthy habits can significantly reduce your risk of stroke and improve overall well-being.

Healthy Eating for Stroke Prevention

A balanced diet plays a crucial role in maintaining cardiovascular health

Fruits & Vegetables

Aim for 5-9 servings daily. Rich in antioxidants and potassium which help lower blood pressure.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Found in fatty fish, flaxseeds, and walnuts. Help reduce inflammation and improve cholesterol.

Whole Grains

Choose whole wheat, oats, quinoa, and brown rice for fiber that helps control cholesterol.

Limit Sodium

Aim for less than 2,300mg daily (1,500mg if you have high blood pressure).

Physical Activity Recommendations

Aerobic Exercise

150 minutes per week of moderate activity (brisk walking, swimming) or 75 minutes of vigorous activity.

Strength Training

2-3 days per week focusing on major muscle groups.

Flexibility & Balance

Yoga or tai chi 2-3 times per week to improve coordination and prevent falls.

Other Healthy Habits

Quit Smoking

Smoking doubles stroke risk by damaging blood vessels.

Limit Alcohol

No more than 1 drink per day for women, 2 for men.

Quality Sleep

7-9 hours per night to allow brain recovery.

Stress Management

Chronic stress increases stroke risk - practice relaxation techniques.
