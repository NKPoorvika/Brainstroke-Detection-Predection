Brain Health

Maintaining a healthy brain is essential for preventing strokes and ensuring long-term cognitive function.

Ways to Keep Your Brain Healthy

Simple lifestyle changes can significantly improve your brain health and reduce stroke risk.

Mental Stimulation

Engage in activities that challenge your brain like puzzles, reading, or learning new skills.

Physical Exercise

Regular physical activity increases blood flow to the brain and promotes new brain cell growth.

Healthy Diet

Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and omega-3 fatty acids to nourish your brain.

Quality Sleep

7-8 hours of quality sleep helps clear brain toxins and consolidate memories.

Social Connection

Strong social ties reduce stress and may lower dementia risk.

Stress Management

Chronic stress can damage brain cells - practice relaxation techniques.

Stroke Risk Factors Affecting Brain Health

High Blood Pressure

The leading cause of stroke. Keep your blood pressure below 120/80.

Diabetes

Doubles stroke risk. Maintain healthy blood sugar levels.

Smoking

Damages blood vessels and accelerates brain aging.

Obesity

Excess weight increases stroke risk by 64%.
